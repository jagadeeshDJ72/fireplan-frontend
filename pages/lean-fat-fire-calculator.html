What is a Lean Fat FIRE Calculator?

Not all FIRE is the same. Depending on the lifestyle you want in retirement, your required corpus varies dramatically. This calculator compares four popular FIRE variants — Lean, Regular, Fat, and Barista — so you can choose the right target for your retirement vision.

Lean FIRE: Retire on minimal expenses (₹30,000-50,000/month). Frugal lifestyle, small town, minimal luxury. Corpus needed: ₹1-2 crore.

Regular FIRE: Maintain current lifestyle. Most popular choice. Corpus: ₹2-4 crore for most Indian families.

Fat FIRE: Retire with luxury — business class travel, premium healthcare, city living. ₹1-2 lakh/month. Corpus: ₹4-8 crore.

Barista FIRE: Semi-retire. Earn some income through part-time work or freelancing, covering 30-50% of expenses. Significantly reduces corpus needed.

How is it calculated?

For each FIRE type, corpus is calculated using PV of inflation-adjusted expenses:

Corpus = Inflation-adjusted annual expense × (1 − (1+r)^−n) ÷ r

Where r = real return (post-retirement return − inflation), n = years in retirement

• Lean FIRE: Base expenses × 60%
• Regular FIRE: Base expenses × 100%
• Fat FIRE: Base expenses × 200%
• Barista FIRE: Base expenses × 70% (assumes 30% income from part-time work)

Frequently asked questions

Which FIRE type is most popular in India?▾
Regular FIRE is the most common goal — maintaining current lifestyle without working. Barista FIRE is growing in popularity among those who enjoy meaningful work and want financial security without full retirement.
Can I upgrade from Lean to Fat FIRE later?▾
Yes — start with Lean FIRE, enjoy semi-retirement, and continue some income. As your investments grow, you can upgrade to Regular or Fat FIRE. Many FIRE seekers find they spend less than expected in retirement, making Lean FIRE more comfortable than anticipated.
