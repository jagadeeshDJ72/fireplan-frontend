What is a SWP Depletion Calculator?

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) allows you to withdraw a fixed amount regularly from your mutual fund corpus. The SWP depletion calculator answers the critical retirement question: given my corpus and monthly withdrawal, for how many years will my money last before running out?

This calculator also shows you the concept of the Immortal Corpus — the corpus size where your returns permanently exceed your withdrawals, meaning your principal never depletes. This is the ultimate FIRE goal.

How is it calculated?

The corpus depletes when withdrawals exceed returns:

Year-end balance = Opening balance × (1 + return) − Annual withdrawal × (1 + inflation)^year

The corpus depletes when this balance hits zero.

The Immortal Corpus threshold:
Immortal Corpus = Annual withdrawal ÷ (Return rate − Inflation rate)

If your corpus exceeds this threshold, the principal never depletes — you withdraw returns only.

How to use this calculator

1. Enter your starting corpus — your total retirement savings
2. Enter monthly withdrawal — your expected monthly expenses in retirement
3. Set expected return — post-retirement portfolio return (7-9% for balanced allocation)
4. Set inflation — your withdrawals increase with inflation each year
5. Calculator shows years until depletion and the Immortal Corpus threshold for your withdrawals

Benefits

• Reality check: Know if your corpus will outlast you or run out too soon
• Find the right corpus: Adjust inputs to find the corpus that lasts your desired retirement period
• Immortal threshold: Know exactly how much more corpus you need to never run out
• Year-by-year table: See your exact balance every year in retirement

Frequently asked questions

What is a safe withdrawal rate for India?▾
India's safe withdrawal rate is 3-3.5% annually (vs 4% in US) due to higher inflation. For a ₹2 crore corpus, this means ₹50,000-58,000/month is sustainable indefinitely. Above this, corpus depletes over time.
How does inflation affect SWP?▾
If you start withdrawing ₹50,000/month and inflation is 6%, you need ₹89,000/month after 10 years to maintain the same lifestyle. Your portfolio must earn above inflation to sustain increasing withdrawals.
What asset allocation is best for post-retirement SWP?▾
50% equity (Nifty 50 index funds) + 30% FD/bonds + 20% gold/REITs is a common post-retirement allocation. This balances inflation-beating equity returns with stability. SWP from debt/FD first, replenish from equity when markets are up.
